c. 190–160 BC

Founder of the Artaxiad dynasty who unified Greater Armenia, founded Artaxata (Plutarch credits Hannibal with helping lay out the city), and set bilingual boundary stones across the realm.

  • 13Held the kingdom together after Seleucid collapse without overplaying his hand against Rome or Parthia.
  • 15Won the wars that made independence possible; a founder-king who fought as well as ruled.
  • 16Capital-building and territorial organisation are his main legacy — the strongest of his five stats.
  • 10Sources praise state-building more than court plots.
  • 13Hellenistic patronage and lawgiving, but not a scholar-king.

Why B tier: Total 67 — a capable founder with excellent stewardship, but not a empire-scale figure like Tigranes.

Tigranes II Artaxiad S tier

Տիգրան Բ Արտաշեսյան

95–55 BC

King of Kings who forged the largest Armenian empire — Caspian to Mediterranean — built Tigranocerta, and only yielded to Rome after Lucullus and Pompey broke his outer provinces.

  • 17Alliances with Pontus, Parthia, and Judea; survived as client king after defeat.
  • 18Decades of successful expansion before the Roman hammer fell — the apex of Armenian military power.
  • 17New capital, populated cities, and a treasury that matched his ambitions.
  • 14Managed a multi-ethnic court; not a plotter, but no fool either.
  • 14Hellenistic court culture at Tigranocerta, though war overshadows letters.

Why S tier: Total 80 — the only king here who clearly clears the “legendary” bar on multiple axes at once.

Արտավազդ Բ Արտաշեսյան

55–34 BC

A playwright-king who wrote tragedies in Greek, balanced Rome and Parthia for years, then was betrayed by Antony, paraded in golden chains, and executed by Cleopatra.

  • 14Kept independence between two superpowers until the Antony disaster.

Տրդատ Ա Արշակունի

63–88 AD

Founder of Arsacid Armenia and a Zoroastrian magus whose coronation by Nero in Rome — after a journey with 3,000 retainers — is one of Tacitus’s great set-pieces.

  • 16The Nero coronation settlement ended decades of Roman–Parthian war over Armenia.
  • 13Magus first, general second; power came from negotiation.
  • 13Established a dynasty that lasted centuries.
  • 12Navigated Parthian and Roman interests without major scandal.
  • 14Religious learning as a magus counts here.

Tiridates III Arsacid B tier

Տրդատ Գ Արշակունի

c. 298–330 AD

Made Armenia the first Christian state (trad. 301 AD), converted by Gregory the Illuminator — the founding event of Armenian Christian historiography.

  • 14Recovered his throne with Roman help and then steered a religious revolution.
  • 16Tradition credits him with legendary physical strength and battlefield prowess.
  • 14Laid the institutional groundwork for a Christian kingdom.
  • 11Agathangelos frames him as a convert, not a schemer.
  • 13Patron of the Church more than a scholar himself.

Why B tier: Total 68 — civilisational importance is unmatched, but the CK3 stats reflect a pious warrior-king rather than a universal genius.
